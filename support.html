Helping Neighbors and loved ones

1) Making Contact
  • Contact the person to confirm your visit
  • Phone the person when you arrive outside their home
  • Complete hand hygenie before leaving your vehicle
  • Ring doorbell or knock loudly and step 2 meters back
  • Inform the perosn who you are, the reason for the visit
2) Once you arrive
  • Ask the person to move to the other room
  • Place items inside the front door
  • If essential follow the guidlines on washing hands and keep a social distance from the person
3) When you leave
  • Confirm time and date of next visit
  • Reassure the person that contact and deleiverys will be maintained while they remain at home
  • Complete hand hygenie prior to returning to vehicle
  • Telephone or email confirmation of visit and wellbeing of person
4) What if the perosn is unwell?
  • If the person reports felling unwell ask if the person has contacted GP
  • If the perosn has not contacted GP, obtain the name and number of GP and do so on their behalf
  • If the GP can't be contacted, phone emergency services
5) What if the person is frail and needs help delivery?
  • Identity location of kitchen and bring in goods/fuel
  • Unload goods ensuring they are accessible for the person
  • Leave the residence and step back 2 metres
  • Reassure the person that contact and deleiveries will be maintained while they remain at home
6) What if there is no response?
  • If no reply, ring contact telephone number
  • If no answer, contact the Local Authority for any additional contact numbers
  • After numerous attempts to contact the person witohut success, phone the emergency services for further assistance
  • Remain at the residence until the emergency services attend
  • Contact the Local Authority with outcome ans update on person
